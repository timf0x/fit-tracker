Exercise Icon Design System

3 Proposals — Onset Fitness

1

Anatomical Silhouette Badges

Each icon features a stylized human silhouette performing the movement with the targeted muscle group highlighted in the body-part color. The silhouette is a simplified, geometric figure — not realistic, but instantly recognizable. The highlighted muscle creates a "heat signature" effect that tells you both what you do and what it works at a glance. Think Olympic sport pictograms meets muscle anatomy charts.

2

Isolated Muscle Glyph Badges

Each icon is a stylized, isolated muscle group shape — not a full body, just the targeted muscles rendered as abstract organic forms. A small equipment symbol (barbell, dumbbell, cable) is overlaid in the corner as a secondary indicator. The muscle shapes use gradient fills that glow from the center, creating a "living anatomy" feel. Each body part has its own organic silhouette that becomes instantly recognizable through repetition.

3

Neon Movement Arcs

Abstract and geometric — each icon represents the movement path and force direction of the exercise as glowing neon arcs and lines. A small body-part dot (colored circle) anchors the movement, while the arcs trace the path your limbs take. Think of it as a motion capture trail frozen in a single frame. The result is minimalist, futuristic, and uniquely "Onset". No other fitness app uses this approach — it's pure movement language.
