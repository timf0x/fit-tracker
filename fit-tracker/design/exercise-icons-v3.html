Exercise Icons v3

Serious & Premium — 3 Proposals

Proposal A

Bold Muscle Maps

Anatomical cross-section style. Each icon shows the targeted muscle group as a bold, abstract muscle fiber pattern — think medical illustration meets modern UI. No human figures. Just the raw muscle tissue with directional fiber lines, highlighted in the body-part color. The shape of the icon itself hints at the body region (diamond for chest, triangle for back, etc.).

Vibe: Clinical, authoritative, like opening an anatomy textbook designed by Apple.

Proposal B

Kinetic Lines

Movement trajectory visualization. Each icon captures the path of the weight/body through space — the arc of a curl, the vertical press path, the lateral spread of a fly. Bold mono-weight strokes with motion trails and terminal dots marking start/end positions. No human figure needed — the motion itself tells the story.

Vibe: Technical, precise, like a physics diagram from a biomechanics lab.

Instantly readable Very unique Clean at all sizes Movement-focused

Proposal C

Heavy Iron Glyphs

Equipment-centric, bold and heavy. Each icon shows the actual equipment in use — barbell, dumbbell, cable, pull-up bar — rendered with thick, confident strokes and a subtle body-part color accent. The equipment IS the icon. No people, no muscles, just iron. Subtle motion indicators (speed lines, angle marks) differentiate exercises using the same equipment.

Vibe: Raw, industrial, like the branding of a serious powerlifting gym.
